My Mrudangam Journey

Eight years ago, I embarked on a journey that would shape not just my musical abilities, but my understanding of rhythm, mathematics, and dedication. Learning mrudangam under the guidance of my guru has been a transformative experience that extends far beyond the instrument itself.

The mrudangam, often called the "king of percussion" in Carnatic music, is an instrument that demands precision, patience, and deep respect for tradition. From my very first lesson where I learned to position my hands correctly, to now being able to perform complex korvai patterns, every step has been a lesson in discipline and artistry.

What fascinates me most about mrudangam is its mathematical foundation. Each talam (rhythm cycle) follows precise patterns, and traditional compositions often reveal algorithmic structures that parallel concepts in computer science. This intersection of ancient musical tradition and modern computational thinking has become a passion of mine.

Why Mrudangam?

The mrudangam chose me as much as I chose it. The intricate hand techniques, the rich tonal variations between the left and right sides of the drum, and the endless possibilities for creative expression within structured talam frameworks drew me in completely. It's an instrument where tradition and innovation coexist beautifully.
